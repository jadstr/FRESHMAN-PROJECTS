Former Philippine president Rodrigo Duterte once infamously likened himself to Hitler, declaring he "would be happy to slaughter" the millions of drug addicts that he said was destroying his country. During his presidency from 2016 to 2022, Duterte styled himself as an anti-drug crusader, whose ruthless "war on drugs" killed thousands of alleged dealers and addicts. His is a contentious legacy. A hugely popular leader, he was also fiercely opposed and criticised for his sexist comments, allegations of sexual abuse and other human rights violations. His anti-drug campaign, in particular, drew international condemnation. In March 2025, Philippine police arrested the 79-year-old after the International Criminal Court issued a warrant accusing him of crimes against humanity over the deadly campaign. Human rights monitors called it a historic moment that marks the beginning of accountability for the thousands of deaths under his watch. Days before his arrest, Duterte had said he was ready to go to jail. "If that's my fate, that's fine I will accept it," he had said.
